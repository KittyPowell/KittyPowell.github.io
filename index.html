About Galois Blog

Galois blog is a website I created for Bright Network's 2024 Couch to coder course on basic HTML and CSS. My hope is for this to become a place for me to write regular blog posts about all aspects of my life as a maths student, university life and short articles on interesting pieces of mathematics.

I've named this blog after Evariste Galois, who is my favourite mathematician.

Who is Galois?

Galois (1811-1832) is a mathematician with a fascinating life story. Not only did he come up with a develop a beautiful and new way to think about symmetry and solving polynomials, he was politically involved as a republican (at the time of Les Misérables), imprisoned for conspiracy to overthrow the government and died tragically in a duel at the age of 20, possibly due to a love affair he'd had with the daughter of a physician he knew.

"Ne pleure pas, Alfred ! J'ai besoin de tout mon courage pour mourir à vingt ans!" - Galois's last words (spoken to his brother), 1832
Translation: "Don’t weep, Alfred! I need all my courage to die at twenty!"

Letter Galois wrote before the night of his duel, outlining his mathematical ideas.
